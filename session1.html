Introduction

As previously mentioned, knitted fabrics have a variety of end-uses, such as dress wear bed linen upholstery, carpets etc. Knitting has developed into an important method of fabric manufacture and competes with woven fabrics for new share in the market. You Learned in the previous unit that woven fabric are made by interlacing of two sets of yarns, warp and weft. In knitted fabrics, usually we find only one set of yarns. In the Formation of knitted fabrics, yarns are formed into loops first and then these loops are interconnected. Depending on whether the set of yarn used for loop formation is lying in length- wise or cross-wise direction of the fabric produced, we distinguish between warp (length-wise) yearn while weft knitted fabrics are produced with a set of weft (cross-wise) yarns. Interconnected loops made from warps/ weft yarns are the structural elements of knitted fabrics. In this session, I wish to explain you how knitted fabric are made. further, the most imported technical terms associated with knitted fabric formation will be described.
